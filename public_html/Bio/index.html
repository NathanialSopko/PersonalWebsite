A little bit about me:

Loving everything about computers, naturally, I attended University at Buffalo in New York from 2014 to 2018 to earn my bachelors degree in Computer Science, graduating with honors. Most of my time spent out of school was working either one of the three technical internships or landscaping. However, my other passion is, in broad terms, anything on two wheels, more specifically motorcycles - street and dirt.

Among the projects that I have worked on, the idea of full-stack development peaked my interest. Seeing something through, from start to finish, database to web app, was incredibly satisfying. Similar situations, as in mobile development are equally as challenging and rewarding. To learn more about work related projects, please visit the 'Experience' tab in the navigation bar.

Current personal projects involve using an esp8266 Arduino module to control power to an outlet. This is done with relays, controlled by the Arduino module, which is in turn controlled by a web server. The goal is to be able to turn any outlet on or off in my bedroom remotely via web application or mobile app.

Areas of interest for future projects include learning media editing and further research into front end development. It is frustrating to reach a step where you have built something very powerful, but don't have the skill-set to make it user friendly or appealing in any way. Frameworks have aided a lot of my design in the past, but I would like to build off of them or develop more custom user interfaces in the future.
